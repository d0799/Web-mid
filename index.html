Albert Einstein was a German-born physicist who is widely considered to be one of the greatest physicists of all time. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity, but he also made important contributions to quantum mechanics. Einstein is also famous for his massâ€“energy equivalence formula E = mc2. He received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect, which was a crucial step in the development of quantum theory. Einstein's work has had a profound impact on our understanding of the universe. His theory of relativity has revolutionized the way we think about space, time, and gravity. Quantum mechanics, which Einstein also played a role in developing, is the foundation of our understanding of the atomic and subatomic world. Einstein was also a vocal advocate for peace and social justice. He was a founding member of the Emergency Committee of Atomic Scientists, which warned about the dangers of nuclear weapons. Einstein's legacy continues to inspire people around the world.
